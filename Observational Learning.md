“There are four processes that researchers have identified as influencing observational learning: attention, retention, reproduction, and motivation” (Debell, 2021)

# Attention


To learn, people need to watch their surroundings, and how much they pay attention can change depending on who they're learning from and where they are learning from.

When it comes to observational learning, there are various factors that influence the extent to which individuals pay attention to the behaviors of others. One of these factors is the degree of similarity between the model and the observer. People are more likely to pay attention to individuals who they perceive as similar to themselves in some way. This could be in terms of status, talent, intelligence, or any other characteristic that the observer finds relatable.

Additionally, the observer's current mood can also affect their level of attention. If someone is in a positive mood or feeling motivated, they may be more inclined to focus and pay closer attention to the actions of others. On the other hand, if someone is in a negative mood or feeling distracted, their attention may be diminished.

Bandura (1985) proposed these ideas, highlighting the significance of these variables in the process of observational learning. By understanding how these factors come into play, we can gain insights into why certain models or behaviors may capture our attention more than others.

# Retention 

Attention alone is not sufficient to learn a new behavior. Observers must also retain, or remember, the behavior for later. To enhance retention, observers can organize the information in a manner that is easy to remember.

# Ok, Now how do i use this?
#### 1. **Watch Pro Players**
- **Identify Top Players**: Watch high-level players, especially those who excel with the same Heroes you use. Note their positioning, decision-making, and mechanical skills (which is more than just aim). 
- **Analyze Their Tactics**: Pay attention to how they engage in fights, use abilities, and move around the map. Try to understand the reasoning behind their choices. See the value it brings to them and their team.
- **Emulate Their Playstyle**: Implement the tactics and strats you observe into your own gameplay. Start by replicating specific moves or decisions in similar situations.
#### 2. **Review Your Own Gameplay**
- **Record or Stream Your Gameplay**: If you are able to record or stream, record to your drive if you have the space, and Stream to twitch or YouTube (YouTube lets you save and keep your VODs up for as long as your channel exists), if you are scared of the public eye, you can do unlisted streams, and then no one can see you!
- **Compare with Pro Gameplay**: After watching your matches, compare your decisions with those of the pros in similar situations. Identify what they did differently and why it worked.
- **Identify Patterns**: Look for recurring mistakes or missed opportunities in your gameplay. Observational learning is about recognizing patterns and adjusting your behavior accordingly. There is a feeling you can get when you are spectating your own gameplay versus when you're actually playing. When you are watching your own gameplay back you notice things you may not have in the moment and can learn to start noticing these things in the moment. 
#### 3. **Join Communities and Discuss Strats**
- **Ask for Feedback:** Share your gameplay clips and ask for feedback from more experienced players. Observing their critiques can help you see aspects of your gameplay that you might have missed. Don't take things personally, the people who actually want to help you will actually help more than you know. Remember they don't know you personally, they are judging the clips solely off of the gameplay and not the person playing it. 
- **Where to ask for feedback:** Most servers have a "share my clips" section, ~~but we plan if we get the~~ 
#### 4. **Focus on Specific Skills**
- **Aim and Movement**: Watch how top players use their mechanics and movement, especially in intense firefights. Try to incorporate their techniques into your practice routines.
- **Ability Usage**: When watching people that play the same Heroes as you, pay attention to the way and timing of how they use their abilities in a fight.
#### 5. **Watch Competitive Tourneys and Events**
- **Follow Official Tournaments**: Watch ALGS, high tier Tourneys, and other high tier events. The Strats used in high stakes environments can be especially useful.
- **Analyze Team Play**: Observe how teams coordinate, rotate, and communicate. Team dynamics and decision making under pressure are critical aspects that can be learn from watching competitive play. Also note how they use nonverbal communication within their team, this will improve your ability to help the team win the game when you don't have coms in game. Nonverbal communication comes in many different forms, but the most common in Marvel Rivals are as follows: Pings and a lack of support (healing and damage from your dps and strategists respectfully).
#### 6. **Practice What You Observe**
- **Set Small Goals**: After observing a specific tactic, try to apply it in your next few matches. For example, if you noticed that a pro player's method of rotating through the map, try to mimic that in your games.
- **Refine Through Repetition**: Observational Learning is most effective when combined with practice. Repeat the strats and techniques you've learned until they become second nature.
